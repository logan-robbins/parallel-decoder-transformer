\section{Architecture}

PDT turns one frozen decoder into $K$ coordinated output streams. Its central design decision is that decomposition and cross-stream state are \textbf{model-internal}.

\subsection{Frozen trunk topology}

We initialize PDT from Qwen3-4B Base (36 layers, $d_{\text{model}} = 2560$, GQA 32 query heads / 8 KV heads) and freeze all parameters. Trainable parameters $\phi$ are introduced as a lightweight coordination stack:
\[
\Theta = \theta_{\text{pre}} \cup \phi.
\]

The trainable subset $\phi$ follows parameter-efficient adaptation traditions \cite{hu2021lora,houlsby2019parameter}. It contains four components: per-layer stream adapters and per-layer SNC cross-attention on every instrumented trunk layer (we instrument every third layer, indices $\{2, 5, 8, \ldots, 35\}$); planner and notes modules (a fixed-slot planner head, a shared plan-embedding matrix, a per-stream snapshot-0 projection, a notes head, and a speculation head); and auxiliary control heads (coverage, agreement, stream classifier).

\paragraph{Symmetry breaking at round 1.} Although every stream receives the same prompt tokens, stream identity $k$ enters the forward pass through three compounded mechanisms: (i) each instrumented decoder layer hosts a dedicated bottleneck adapter with independently-initialized weights, routed by stream identifier; (ii) snapshot 0 is \emph{per-stream seeded}---each stream's slot of snapshot 0 is a projection of only the plan items the planner assigned to that stream, under a disjoint-ownership invariant; and (iii) each stream maintains its own Dynamic Notes Bus window and KV cache.

\subsection{Prompt-time latent planner}

Let $\mathbf{H}_x \in \mathbb{R}^{T \times d}$ denote the frozen-trunk hidden states of the prompt. The planner performs a mandatory planning pass before any output tokens:
\[
\mathbf{\Pi} = \mathrm{PlannerHead}(\mathrm{MaskedMeanPool}(\mathbf{H}_x)) \in \mathbb{R}^{S \times V_p}, \qquad z_i = \arg\max_{a} \mathbf{\Pi}_{i,a}.
\]
In our configuration $S = 16$ and $V_p = 8{,}192$. We reduced $V_p$ from $65{,}536$ to $8{,}192$ to bring the planner-head parameter count from ${\sim}3$B to ${\sim}336$M at $d_{\text{model}} = 2560$; Stage 0 training is gated on codebook-utilization diagnostics (Section~\ref{sec:curriculum}) so collapse surfaces before scaling back up.

Active slots are re-embedded and projected \emph{per stream} into notes space:
\[
\mathbf{e}_i = E_{\text{plan}}[z_i], \qquad
\mathbf{n}^{\text{plan},(k)}_0 = \mathrm{LayerNorm}\!\left(W_{\text{plan}} \cdot \mathrm{pool}_{i \in \mathrm{own}(k)} \mathbf{e}_i\right),
\]
where $\mathrm{own}(k)$ is the disjoint subset of planner slots owned by stream $k$. This per-stream seeding is the first symmetry-breaking mechanism.

\subsection{Dynamic Notes Bus as shared latent workspace}

The \textbf{Dynamic Notes Bus} is PDT's shared latent workspace: an embeddings-only, versioned FIFO of per-stream snapshots, one bus per stream. Textual plans and rendered notes may be used for dataset construction, observability, or supervision, but inference-time coordination uses only note embeddings.

We describe the runtime at two timescales: $\tau$ is tokens per provisional block between synchronization decisions ($\tau = 32$), and $\Delta$ is the reveal delay between emission and visibility to siblings ($\Delta = 1$). The visible notes window for stream $k$ at round $v$ is
\[
\mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v = \mathrm{Window}\!\left(\{\mathbf{n}^{\text{plan},(k)}_0\} \cup \{\widehat{\mathbf{n}}^{(j)}_u : j \in [K],\; u \le v-\Delta\}\right).
\]

\subsection{Speculative Note Conditioning}

SNC is the read mechanism over the visible workspace. At each decode step $u$ inside round $v$, stream $k$ conditions on $\mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v$. The SNC projections use learned parameters independent of trunk weights:
\[
\mathbf{Q}^{(k)}_{v,u} = \mathbf{H}^{(k)}_{v,u} W_Q, \qquad
\mathbf{K}^{(k)}_v = \mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v W_K, \qquad
\mathbf{V}^{(k)}_v = \mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v W_V,
\]
so Qwen3's GQA does not collide with SNC's symmetric multi-head layout. Each instrumented layer applies the resulting context through a trust-gated residual:
\[
\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}^{(k)}_{v,u} = \mathbf{H}^{(k)}_{v,u} + \sigma(\lambda_l) \cdot \mathrm{SNC}_l(\mathbf{H}^{(k)}_{v,u}, \mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v) \, W_O,
\]
where $\lambda_l$ is a per-layer pre-sigmoid gate parameter initialized to $-4.0$ so $\sigma(\lambda_l) \approx 0.018$ at step 0.

\subsection{Provisional writes, coverage, readiness}

At the end of each provisional block, stream $k$ writes a latent summary back to its own bus via the speculation head:
\[
\widehat{\mathbf{n}}^{(k)}_v = \mathrm{SpeculationHead}(\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}^{(k)}_{v,\tau}).
\]
Coverage is predicted against the planner-seeded plan catalog:
\[
\mathbf{c}^{(k)}_v = \mathrm{CoverageHead}(\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}^{(k)}_{v,\tau}, E_{\text{plan}}(z_{1:S}), m_{\text{plan}}).
\]

\subsection{Agreement-gated commit}

Per the paper contract, the readiness score for stream $k$ at round $v$ is a function of four inputs:
\[
r^{(k)}_v = \mathrm{AgreeHead}\!\left(\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}^{(k)}_{v,\tau},\ \mathcal{W}^{(k)}_v,\ \mathbf{c}^{(k)}_v,\ \widehat{\mathbf{n}}^{(k)}_v\right).
\]
The implementation attends over the notes window with the block-end hidden as a query, encodes coverage via a compact statistic, projects the stream's own provisional note, concatenates all four features, and scores. The threshold $\gamma$ is tuned from a held-out ROC sweep, not learned. The global continuation rule is $A_v = \mathbf{1}\!\left[\min_{k} r^{(k)}_v > \gamma\right]$.

\section{Staged Training Curriculum}
\label{sec:curriculum}

Training a coordination mechanism on a frozen trunk is unstable if all modules are enabled at once. PDT uses a four-stage curriculum: Stage~0 (planner pretrain) trains the planner head, plan embedding, plan-notes projection, and notes head. Stage~1 (stream bootstrap) unfreezes per-layer adapters, per-layer SNC, and the stream classifier. Stage~2 (bus enablement) unfreezes the speculation head. Stage~3 (commit control) unfreezes coverage and agreement. Stage gating is explicit in the training loop: LM CE/KD activate at Stage~2+; coverage and readiness BCE activate at Stage~3+.

\paragraph{Codebook-utilization diagnostics (Stage 0 gate).} Stage~0 logs four statistics: unique entries used (fraction of $V_p$ selected at least once), per-slot entropy in bits (Shannon entropy of each slot's selection distribution), pairwise anchor cosine (similarity between per-stream snapshot-0 anchors on the same prompt), and the usage histogram. The Stage 0 $\to$ Stage 1 gate is $\texttt{unique\_entries} \ge 1000$ AND $\max_s \texttt{entropy}(s) \ge 2$ bits.

\section{Falsifiable Existence-Proof Experiment}
\label{sec:falsifiability}

With the SNC-conditioned hidden
\[
\widetilde{\mathbf{H}}^{(k)}_{v,u} = \mathbf{H}^{(k)}_{v,u} + \sigma(\lambda_l) \cdot \mathrm{softmax}\!\left(\tfrac{Q^{(k)}_{v,u} K^{(k)\top}_v}{\sqrt{d}}\right) V^{(k)}_v W_O,
\]
the coordination claim is
\[
\exists\, k \ne j,\ v,\ u \le v-\Delta:\quad \frac{\partial\, p(\hat y^{(k)}_{v,t})}{\partial\, \widehat{\mathbf{n}}^{(j)}_{u}} \ne \mathbf{0},
\]
and this partial derivative vanishes whenever $\sigma(\lambda_l) = 0$ at every instrumented layer.

\paragraph{Primary hypothesis.} $K$ streams with trained SNC produce outputs with measurably higher cross-stream differentiation than $K$ streams with ablated SNC, measured by pairwise cosine distance between per-stream outputs, cross-stream ROUGE-L (lower = more differentiated), Jensen--Shannon divergence of entity distributions, contradiction rate, and coverage overlap.

\paragraph{Pre-registered interventions.} \textbf{A---SNC gate ablation.} Force $\sigma(\lambda_l) \leftarrow 0$ at every instrumented layer (implemented by the runtime's force-gate override, which collapses the SNC delta to exactly zero regardless of the trained gate). \textbf{B---Norm-matched sibling-write scramble.} Replace sibling notes with Gaussian vectors rescaled to the empirical per-note norm, isolating informational content from attention-softmax numerics. \textbf{C---Anchor swap.} Replace stream $k$'s snapshot-0 anchor with one drawn from a \emph{different prompt entirely}.

\paragraph{Pre-registered substantiation.} An absolute delta of at least 3 points in coverage overlap or cross-stream contradiction rate between the trained-gate baseline and Intervention A or B rejects the null that SNC is decorative under the current training contract.

\paragraph{Pre-registered null handling.} If Interventions A, B, and C all produce null results, the paper reports that coordination under the current training contract is produced by per-stream adapters and planner seeds alone, and the \emph{informational-necessity} training contract (sibling-citing targets) is named as the next experimental regime. We do not pivot the architecture; we report the null honestly.

\section{Beyond Text: Long-Horizon Frame}

The same mechanism extends directly to three $K$-unrelated-inputs domains where the partition is given rather than inferred. \textbf{Multi-modal fusion}: $K$ modality-specific encoders produce concurrent streams whose outputs must refer to each other in concept-space; modality identity provides built-in symmetry breaking, so the planner may become optional. \textbf{Sensor fusion in robotics}: $K$ sensor channels produce streams that drive $K$ motor channels; a left arm reaching for object A needs to know \emph{what region of workspace the right arm is occupying}, not the right arm's full trajectory. \textbf{Ensemble reasoning}: $K$ reasoning strategies run concurrently on the same problem, each reading sibling concept-state so strategies can diverge to unexplored regions or converge on agreement---distinct from self-consistency's independent rollouts. All three instantiate the same primitive: low-bandwidth, concept-level awareness between $K$ concurrent producers that do not share a high-bandwidth channel.
