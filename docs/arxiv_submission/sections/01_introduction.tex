\section{Introduction}

A foundational bottleneck in large language model (LLM) inference is the sequential, left-to-right nature of autoregressive decoding. While causal masking is fundamental to this paradigm, it forces a trade-off between latency and reasoning depth: complex tasks require long chains of thought, but long chains incur prohibitive latency.

Recent research has attempted to bypass this bottleneck through ``around-the-model'' parallelization. Techniques like Skeleton-of-Thought (SoT) \cite{ning2023skeleton} decompose tasks into sub-questions and prompt the model to answer them in parallel. While promising, these methods treat the model as a black box, leading to a critical failure mode we term \textbf{Coherence Drift}: because parallel streams cannot communicate, they often hallucinate conflicting facts or redundant content.

We address this by moving both planning and coordination \textit{inside} the model. We introduce the \textbf{Parallel Decoder Transformer (PDT)}, a decoder-only architecture whose central mechanism is a mandatory prompt-time latent planning pass followed by a shared embeddings-only coordination bus. The planner seeds a shared latent state at $t=0$, and downstream streams condition on that state through SNC cross-attention rather than waiting for a textual plan string. The resulting coordination is implemented by a stack: the planner provides the initial latent code, while plan projection, note emission, SNC consumption, and rollback heads maintain the shared state during decoding. The entire coordination stack attaches to a frozen backbone via lightweight sidecar modules, requiring no modification of pre-trained weights.

\subsection{Contributions}
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Planner-first latent coordination stack.} A mandatory prompt-time planner predicts fixed latent plan slots over a shared vocabulary, re-embeds those slots into notes space, and broadcasts the resulting seed as snapshot~0 on an embeddings-only Dynamic Notes Bus before any stream emits tokens. SNC, note-emission, coverage, and agreement modules then consume and update that workspace during decoding.
    \item \textbf{Versioned embedding bus semantics.} SNC reads lagged note embeddings every decode step, while cadence and agreement logic control when new snapshots are written or when rollback is triggered. The bus carries embeddings only; textual plans and notes are auxiliary artifacts for dataset construction and scoring.
    \item \textbf{Implementation-aligned dataset and training contract.} The dataset writes a real snapshot-0 plan seed, hashes canonical plan items into the planner vocabulary, and masks LM supervision so each stream trains only on its own section.
\end{enumerate}

Quantitative evaluation of the revised coordination stack is in progress and will appear in a future revision.
